The Practical Benefits of Piano Training
	The ability to play the piano, and being trained in music in general, 
yield many positive results for both beginning and lifelong students.
This <a href='http://www.popsci.com/science/article/2013-02/your-brain-music-infographic'>charming infographic</a>
sums up a few effects, but there are many more to be found.
Studies <a href='http://scholar.google.com/scholar?hl=en&q=benefit+of+music+lesson&btnG=&as_sdt=1%2C45'>such as these</a> 
or <a href='http://scholar.google.com/scholar?q=differences+of+pianists&btnG=&hl=en&as_sdt=1%2C45'>or these</a> 
have shown such benefits as:
<ul>
<li>Increased sensitivity to emotion in sound (read <a href='http://abcnews.go.com/Technology/DyeHard/story?id=7050081&page=1#.UWxXIopDulo'>this fascinating article</a>!).</li>
<li>Creativity and interdisciplinary innovation (some more <a href='http://www.npr.org/blogs/deceptivecadence/2013/04/09/176681242/can-yo-yo-ma-fix-the-arts'>interesting reading</a>)</li>
<li>Ability to learn new tasks, as well as IQ.</li>
<li>Auditory and visual reaction time.</li>
<li>Dexterity and motor skills.</li>
<li>Gray matter density.</li>
<li>Cerebellum and Corpus Callosum size.</li>
<li>Equality of brain hemisphere development.</li>
<li>Connection between auditory and motor regions.</li>
<li>And with the following especially true in children: <ul>
<li>Speech related pitch discrimination.</li>
<li>Reading.</li><li>Vocabulary.</li>
<li>Verbal sequencing.</li>
<li>Academic performance.</li>
<li>Spatial/temporal intelligence.</li>
<li>Self-esteem.</li>
<li>Maturity of synaptic connections.</li></ul>
</ul>
	Music brings joy to anyone who gives it a chance, and it's never too late to begin! 
Adult piano students report improved self-actualization, enjoyment, personal growth, 
and escape from routine after beginning lessons, as shown by 
<a href='http://jrm.sagepub.com/content/54/2/97.short'>this study</a>.
	Cultivate something beautiful in yourself!
